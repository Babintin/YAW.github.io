Who We Are

Center For Democratic Empowerment is a Civil Society Organization established in Ghana with the aim of;

  • Ensuring a peaceful co-existence of all persons irrespective of their diverse backgrounds.
  • Deepening and strengthening democratic rule in Ghana and beyond.
  • Eradicating Poverty in Ghana.

What We Do

We strive to achieve our goals through our carefully tailored Programs, Events and Projects with the requisite and information technical know how, materials and facilities capable of enhancing our goals and objectives. Most of our activities are centered around the under listed;

  • Human Rights Advocacy

    • Ensuring that people may exercise their rights and that governments are held responsible for defending and advancing human rights.

  • Poverty Eradication

Our Functions

The rights and freedoms of people and groups are something we work to safeguard and advance. By doing research and gathering information on abuses and violations of human rights, we accomplish this. Human rights violations are documented using this data, which is also used to spot trends and patterns. In order to modify the laws, regulations, and practices that infringe on human rights, we use the data acquired. We might also run public education and awareness-building efforts to draw attention to issues relating to human rights.

Victims of violations of human rights receive legal aid from us, and they are also free to file legal complaints in court. The group also offers assistance and services to those who have been subjected to violations of human rights, including social services, legal representation, and medical and psychiatric care.
